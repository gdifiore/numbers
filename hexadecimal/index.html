Convert Binary to Hexadecimal

Take a binary number (we'll use 1110002) and split it into sets of 4 digits, reading from right to left (if it's not split into perfect sets of 4 you can add 0s to fill the empty spots or just leave it)

| 11 | 1000 |

now like in binary to decimal, the right-most digit is 1, second is 2, then 4 and then 8. for each 1 in the binary set add the number that it aligns up with with the others e.g.

| 0 0 1 1 | 1 0 0 0 |
8 4 2 1 | 8 4 2 1


then for each division add all the numbers with a one above it together (but not between divisions)
| 2+1 | 8 |
so 1110002 in hexadecimal is 3816

and if we convert 1110002 to hexadecimal we get 3816
*note that that 3816 is not the same as 3810 in decimal

let's take a hex number, say F416 and convert it to binary
first convert any letters to their number equivalent so F16 is 15

now we have to make 15 and 4 out of 4 binary digits
15 = 11112 which is 8+4+2+1 = 15
and 4 is 0100 which is 4 = 4
so F416 in binary is 111101002

and if we check with my converter F416 is 111101002 in binary.
